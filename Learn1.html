The Development Process

Stage 1: Concept Creation

Where It All Begins

The game development process all starts with a idea, or concept for a game that the creator(s) wants to build. The team will come togehter and brainstorm ideas for something they find, fun, engaging, and interesting. They also want to come up with something unique, and not rehash a common game. They want to create a game that only they could make.

This can last for days or weeks, until the team feels that they have an amazing idea that want to roll with.



Stage 2: Planning and Features

Hitting The Ground Running

What happens after the team has figured out what idea they want to work on? Well they take that awesome idea and begin planning it out! Will be a 2D or 3D game? Multiplayer or Single Player? Story driven, or entertainment driven? With some initial ideas, they begin using the
Agile method of developing software.

  • Agile features continuous development

  • Agile features weekly or bi-weekly meetings to test code, discuss priorities, and rethink old ideas.

  • This means that from the time conception and planning begin, the team is testing and writing code, making small, testable versions of their ideas, to see it they are good or bad.
